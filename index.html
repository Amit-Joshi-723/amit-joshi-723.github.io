About Me

I work with product teams to figure out whether what they built is actually working.

I work with product teams to figure out whether what they built is actually working. That means setting up the right tracking before they launch, building the dashboards they use to make decisions, and sitting in the room when they decide what to build next. There is usually a gap between what a product team thinks is happening and what the data actually shows — my job is to close that gap, and be honest when the numbers say something nobody wants to hear. I have been doing this for 13 years across knowledge platforms, GenAI tools, e-commerce, and a few industries you would not expect analytics to show up in.

At BCG I built the analytics function from scratch — just me and five products at the start, a team of analysts and twenty products by the time it found its shape. The job evolved from pulling reports to being the person teams come to before they build anything. I own the full cycle on the analytics side — defining what to measure, designing the data flows, prototyping the dashboards, shipping them, and training the people who use them. BCG Deliver Impact Award 2023. BCG AI Fluency Ladder L4 — AI Multiplier. MBA in Marketing and Leadership Strategy commencing October 2026.

Before BCG I delivered analytics across sports, cybersecurity, and education at HCL — including sole analytics lead for one of the largest football club sites in EMEA. Working across that many industries early on taught me something useful — the measurement framework matters more than the tool. I have carried that into every project since.

I do not just build the dashboard. I make sure it is measuring the right thing, that people trust the numbers, and that the data actually changes what gets built next.

GenAI Product Analytics Framework
● Live
What to measure when the product itself is AI

Most GenAI products are measured like web apps. This framework argues that the metric that matters is not whether users stayed — it is whether the output they received was good enough to use.

Measure output quality not engagement — a user who re-prompts eight times is not engaged, they are frustrated
Connect survey verbatims to metric anomalies — loyal users filling in surveys are telling you what your dashboard cannot see
Define quality KPIs before launch — without a pre-launch baseline you cannot measure improvement, only current state
Segment by user intent — power users, explorers, and casual users need completely different success metrics

E-Commerce Analytics Framework
● Live
What happens before someone clicks Buy is the whole story

Most e-commerce dashboards show you the sale. This framework is about everything that happens before it — intent signals, abandonment by stage, and the logged-in user personalisation pipeline. Built from analytics delivery across a Premier League football club's merchandising platform and an education course marketplace.

Interest Signal Rate as a leading indicator — before the cart, before the checkout, before the sale
Abandonment split into three stages — each a different problem, each a different fix, never averaged together
Personalisation pipeline: Mixpanel → JQL → DFP → ad served → feedback loop closes
Logged-in vs anonymous conversion gap — the proof that identity-based personalisation is working
